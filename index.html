What HIV Is & Isn’t

  • HIV is a virus that affects the immune system.
  • HIV is not a punishment or moral failure.
  • HIV is not an automatic death sentence.

How It’s Transmitted

HIV can be transmitted through:

  • Unprotected sex
  • Sharing needles
  • Mother-to-child transmission without treatment

It is not transmitted through hugging, sharing food, or shaking hands.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Only certain groups get HIV.

Fact: Anyone can get HIV. It’s about exposure, not identity.

Myth: You can tell if someone has HIV by looking at them.

Fact: You can’t. Only testing can confirm status.

Treatment and Hope

With early testing and consistent treatment, people living with HIV can live long, healthy lives. Medication today can suppress the virus to undetectable levels — meaning it cannot be transmitted.

Common Acronyms and Meanings

  • HIV — Human Immunodeficiency Virus: The virus that attacks the immune system.
  • AIDS — Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome: Advanced stage of HIV infection.
  • PLHIV — People Living with HIV: Preferred inclusive term.
  • ART — Antiretroviral Therapy: Daily medication that keeps HIV under control.
  • U=U — Undetectable = Untransmittable: When viral load is undetectable, HIV cannot be transmitted.
  • PrEP — Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis: Medication to prevent HIV before exposure.
  • PEP — Post-Exposure Prophylaxis: Emergency medication after potential exposure.
  • MSM — Men who have Sex with Men: Neutral epidemiological term, not identity-based.
  • SRH — Sexual and Reproductive Health: Encompasses education, prevention, and care.

Philippine Laws & Protections

The Philippines upholds strong policies to protect people living with HIV, promote awareness, and eliminate discrimination. Knowing these laws helps every citizen and workplace act with dignity and responsibility.

Republic Act No. 8504

(Philippine AIDS Prevention and Control Act of 1998)
  • Prohibits discrimination in employment, education, and travel based on actual or perceived HIV status.
  • Ensures equal access to health care and insurance without extra fees or denial of coverage.
  • Requires confidentiality of HIV-related information and penalizes disclosure without consent.

Read the full law at lawphil.net

Republic Act No. 11166

(Philippine HIV and AIDS Policy Act of 2018)
  • Reinforces non-discrimination in all government and private HIV services.
  • Allows individuals aged 15–17 to undergo HIV testing without parental consent under specific conditions.
  • Mandates PhilHealth and insurers to cover HIV care and treatment.
  • Protects confidentiality, encourages informed disclosure, and strengthens the Philippine National AIDS Council (PNAC).

Read the full law at lawphil.net

Challenges in Implementation

Despite these strong policies, discrimination and limited access to HIV services persist in some workplaces and rural communities. Many organizations still lack the required workplace HIV policy. Sustained awareness, education, and enforcement are key to ensuring these laws truly protect every Filipino.
